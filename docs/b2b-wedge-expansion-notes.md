# Passage B2B Wedge Expansion Notes

Last updated: May 9, 2026

This is a strategic note for after the current funeral-home coordination roadmap is tighter. It should not distract the current sprint loop. Passage should stay deep before it goes wide.

## Current Priority

Finish the core coordination spine for the funeral-home wedge:

- Red path first minutes.
- Family command center.
- Funeral-home director and staff work.
- Participant and vendor loops.
- Prepared outputs and exports.
- Proof, visibility, delivery truth, and pilot instrumentation.

The active mission remains: one family continuity record, one orchestration spine, role-specific views, and real proof of work.

## Later Wedge Thesis

Hospice and senior living / assisted care should be treated as earlier activation points for the same family operating system, not as separate products.

The lifecycle model:

1. Green path: preparation while living.
2. Warm path: decline, hospice, or care-facility transition planning.
3. Red path: immediate death-event coordination.
4. Funeral coordination: service logistics, family communication, and provider handoff.
5. After path: estate, notifications, remembrance, continuity, and long-tail administration.

The strategic insight:

- Funeral homes own the event.
- Hospice owns the transition into the event.
- Senior living and assisted care often own the longer family relationship before the event.
- Families experience all of these as one continuous journey.

Passage should preserve the family workspace across those handoffs.

## Expansion Guardrails

Do not build clinical software.

Avoid:

- EMR replacement.
- Clinical charting.
- Medication administration.
- Symptom tracking.
- Nurse workflow systems.
- Compliance-heavy healthcare claims without review.

Stay focused on:

- Family coordination.
- Role clarity.
- Shared updates.
- Preparedness.
- Practical support.
- Provider handoff.
- Proof and visibility.

## Three B2B Verticals To Test Later

### 1. Funeral Homes

Current wedge.

Positioning: Passage sits on top of existing funeral-home workflow as the family coordination and continuity layer.

Outbound campaign angle:

- Fewer repeated calls.
- Calmer families.
- Shared status.
- Staff delegation.
- Proof and export.
- Modern family experience without replacing Passare, Gather, SRS, or current systems.

### 2. Hospice Locations

Second strategic wedge.

Positioning: Passage is the family coordination layer for end-of-life care transitions. It helps families organize roles, updates, practical support, documents, wishes, and downstream handoff without replacing clinical systems.

Outbound campaign angle:

- Reduce family coordination burden.
- Help caregivers know what to do next.
- Prepare family roles before crisis.
- Keep transition into funeral coordination calmer.
- Give hospice teams a non-clinical family support layer.

### 3. Senior Living / Assisted Care Facilities

Third wedge to contemplate after the funeral-home and hospice loops are clear.

Positioning: Passage helps communities support families through aging transitions, preparedness, emergency contacts, family communication, decline planning, and downstream handoff.

Outbound campaign angle:

- Earlier family trust.
- Lower compliance complexity than clinical hospice workflows.
- Resident wishes and emergency contacts organized before crisis.
- Better family communication during decline or death-event transition.
- Continuity into hospice, funeral home, and aftercare.

## Outbound Campaign System Idea

Eventually, Passage should support an internal outbound campaign workspace for controlled B2B learning across the three verticals.

The workspace should help create and track:

- Target account lists by vertical.
- Contact roles.
- Draft email sequences.
- Demo booking status.
- Pilot interest.
- Agreed free pilots.
- Subscription interest.
- Objections.
- Follow-up date.
- Which vertical is sticking.

Safety boundary: no real emails or SMS should be sent from Passage campaign tooling without explicit approval, sender setup, unsubscribe handling, and compliance review.

## Product Implication

Build the spine now so future verticals can plug into the same lifecycle:

estate / family record -> state -> task -> owner -> communication -> proof -> status -> reporting -> handoff.

The moat is not any single provider category. The moat is persistent family continuity through institutional handoffs.
