# Passage Beta and Demo Operator Plan

## Objective

Use Codex as a low-intervention product/growth operator that keeps Passage moving toward pilots, demos, and customer learning while the owner makes only high-leverage decisions.

## Weekly Rhythm

Monday:
- Review production QA and open risks.
- Pick 3 build tasks and 2 growth tasks.

Wednesday:
- Run persona QA.
- Update demo script and objections.

Friday:
- Summarize what shipped.
- Prepare next outreach assets.
- List owner decisions.

## Demo Assets To Maintain

- Funeral home 15-minute demo script.
- Local one-location demo case.
- Multi-location demo case.
- Family view from funeral home.
- Export CSV walkthrough.
- Act-on-behalf walkthrough.
- Vendor request walkthrough.
- Pricing and pilot offer explanation.

## Demo Readiness Bar

The demo must prove the sell-sheet promise, not merely show screens:

- fewer repeated family calls
- one shared case status
- clear task ownership
- communication tracked automatically
- staff can act on behalf of family
- family sees proof/status without calling
- CSV/export returns data to the funeral home's existing workflow
- vendor help appears only inside relevant tasks and never as a generic directory

If a walkthrough requires explanation that the UI does not support, the path is not demo-ready.

## Funeral Home Sales Narrative

Core promise:
Passage reduces repeated calls, gives families a calmer command center, and lets staff act on behalf of families without losing proof.

Primary buyer demo:
Optimize the next serious funeral-home demo for a one-location owner/director. Multi-location reporting should remain visible as an expansion path, but the first proof story should match the Local-tier buyer: one independent funeral home, stretched staff, repeated family calls, and a need for simple proof/export.

Demo arc:
1. Create or load a case.
2. Add or confirm the staff employee who can own work.
3. Assign staff from the saved employee/case-contact dropdown.
4. Request missing information from the family.
5. Staff handles the task or records what is waiting.
6. Show the family-visible proof/status update.
7. Show report/export change.
8. Explain pilot billing.

The demo should feel like: "holy shit, this actually reduces chaos." Every click should either reduce a call, assign ownership, prepare an output, send/request information, save proof, or update reporting.

## 12-Minute Funeral Home Demo Script

Use this script for the next one-location owner/director demo. Do not pitch Passage as a task manager. Pitch it as a coordination layer that reduces repeated calls and keeps proof visible.

### Minute 0-2: Director Pain

Open the funeral-home dashboard.

Say:
"Most family coordination does not fail because your staff does not care. It fails because information lives in calls, texts, email, memory, and different staff members' notebooks. Passage creates one case spine: who owns the next move, who we are waiting on, what proof exists, and what the family can see."

Show:
- Director operating loop.
- Active cases.
- Estimated calls avoided.
- Staff/delegation and waiting counts.

### Minute 2-4: Case Spine

Open a demo case.

Say:
"This is not a list of tasks. This is the next operational move for the case. Your director sees the full state. Your employee sees assigned work. The family sees reassurance and what we need from them."

Show:
- Case spine next move.
- Owner.
- Next expected update.
- Family-facing status.
- Conversation/proof/notification counts.

### Minute 4-6: Ask Family Once

Use a task where the funeral home needs family input, such as cemetery plot details, pronouncement confirmation, obituary approval, or service preference.

Say:
"Instead of calling the family, texting one person, and asking staff to remember the answer, the request stays attached to the task. Notifications only get attention. The work lives here."

Show:
- Request from family / waiting update path.
- Prepared family message.
- Proof destination.
- No real email/SMS during demo unless explicitly approved.

### Minute 6-8: Staff Handles Work

Open Staff work.

Say:
"Employees land on assigned work first. They do not need the whole business dashboard. They need the case context, owner, expected update, and proof requirement."

Show:
- Add employee profile. No message is sent.
- Return to a case task and assign from the saved employee dropdown.
- My assigned work / Delegation queue.
- Expected update.
- Open work button.
- Record proof / complete output path.

### Minute 8-10: Family Sees Calm Status

Open the family/estate view.

Say:
"The family does not see the operational machinery. They see the next useful thing, who owns it, what is waiting, and proof that something happened. That is what lowers the 'where are we?' calls."

Show:
- Estate operating spine.
- One next move.
- Owner/status/proof rail.
- Coordination trail tucked behind disclosure.

### Minute 10-11: Vendor/Local Support

Open a vendor request path only if relevant.

Say:
"Local support appears only inside a relevant task. It is not a marketplace or ad directory. Vendors get one scoped request and update status so the family and funeral home are not chasing another thread."

Show:
- Scoped vendor request.
- Status/proof trail.
- Estimate language framed as support, not sales.

### Minute 11-12: Reporting / ROI

Return to Reports or export.

Say:
"This fits on top of your existing case system. Passage gives families clarity while giving your team exportable proof, staff workload visibility, and a simple story: fewer repeated calls, clearer ownership, better family experience."

Show:
- CSV/export.
- Calls avoided estimate.
- By-location/by-employee reporting where demo data supports it.

Demo success test:
The director should be able to repeat the value in one sentence: "Passage lets us coordinate with the family and staff in one place, prove what happened, and reduce repeat calls without replacing our system."

## Demo Instance Readiness Checklist

Best-practice demo rule:
Show one painful workflow in action before showing modules. The prospect should see the result in the first few minutes: fewer calls, clearer ownership, visible proof, and exportable reporting.

The demo instance should support these paths without real email, SMS, payments, or production data changes:
- Director creates or opens a realistic case and immediately sees active cases, waiting responses, staff workload, calls avoided, and export/report controls.
- Director adds or confirms one employee, then assigns that employee to one task from the saved staff/case-contact dropdown, with owner, next expected update, proof destination, and family-facing status visible.
- Director asks the family for one missing detail from inside the task, such as cemetery plot details, obituary approval, pronouncement confirmation, or service preference.
- Family sees one calm request, understands why it matters, replies once, and sees the reassuring status change.
- Staff sees assigned work first, handles the task, records proof, and escalates if blocked.
- Participant sees only their delegated responsibility with context, not the full case dashboard.
- Vendor receives only a scoped support request, such as flowers, catering/reception, or transportation, and responds with status/quote without a marketplace feel.
- Director returns to reporting to show calls avoided, tasks handled/waiting, staff workload, CSV export, and proof timeline.

Demo data should include:
- One funeral home with one director/admin, one staff employee, and one location.
- One active case with a family coordinator and at least one participant.
- One task waiting on family input, one assigned staff task, one handled task with proof, and one vendor/support request.
- One export/report surface with believable counts.

Do not over-demo:
- Do not tour every feature.
- Do not start with configuration.
- Do not show raw audit noise before showing human reassurance.
- Do not let vendor support look like ads or lead generation.
- Do not send real messages unless explicitly approved for a controlled test.

Co-branding:
Family command center should be subtly co-branded for funeral-home-created cases: funeral-home logo/accent in the header, small "Powered by Passage" language, and enough Passage identity to support future family planning adoption.

Staff visibility:
Staff default to assigned tasks plus their open cases. Directors/admins and location managers can see all cases at the location or organization scope as appropriate.

## Vendor Network Narrative

Core promise:
Trusted local help appears only when it helps complete a family task.

Rules:
- No directory.
- No ads.
- No endless lists.
- Funeral homes can control preferred partners.
- Passage tracks requests and future revenue opportunities.
- Families and funeral homes can both initiate help, but funeral homes curate/prefer/hide visible options.
- Family requests outside the preferred list route through approval/review.
- First demo categories: flowers/sympathy, catering/reception or meal support, and transportation.
