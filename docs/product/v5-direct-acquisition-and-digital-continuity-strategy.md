# Passage V5 direct acquisition and digital continuity strategy

Status: owner-approved differentiation research packet; non-executable.

This document is a product and distribution strategy beneath the one canonical roadmap, `docs/product/operational-readiness-roadmap.md`. It is not a second roadmap, milestone set, scorecard, launch promise, or authorization to expand Cycle 8. Product sequencing, readiness, release status, and Production authority remain controlled by the canonical roadmap, `AGENTS.md`, and the release train.

Research date: 2026-07-21 (America/Los_Angeles).

## Decision summary

Passage should research a V5 differentiation layer that lets a person or trusted helper begin outside an institution, organize the person's digital continuity, and carry permissioned work into the consumer-directed V4 network without turning Passage into a password vault, executor, funeral marketplace, or grief-content company.

The promising category is not "digital afterlife storage." It is **authority-aware digital continuity**:

```text
know what exists
  -> record the person's intent
  -> name the permitted helper
  -> use each provider's real access or closure process
  -> attach the work to one accountable task
  -> preserve the request, receipt, exception, and recovery owner
  -> return the minimum useful proof to the family
```

The research validates that the underlying job is real and fragmented. It does not yet validate Passage-specific demand, conversion, retention, provider willingness, creator distribution, or willingness to pay. Those remain hypotheses with explicit success and kill criteria below.

## Differentiation thesis

Existing products prove pieces of the job:

- platform-native controls such as Apple Legacy Contact, Google Inactive Account Manager, and Meta Legacy Contact govern one custodian at a time;
- password managers provide credential emergency access but do not coordinate estate authority, provider requests, funeral-home work, or family-safe proof;
- digital-vault and estate-planning products organize documents, assets, household information, and sharing;
- bereavement products guide tasks, cancel accounts, coordinate family members, and distribute through employers and insurers;
- online-first disposition providers digitize arrangements, price selection, chain-of-custody updates, and aftercare.

The whitespace is the continuity between these systems: one permissioned record that says what exists, what the person wanted, who is allowed to act, what the provider requires, what was requested, what succeeded or failed, what proof exists, and who owns recovery. Passage can differentiate by connecting planning, at-need professional care, and aftercare without taking custody of credentials or disintermediating the selected funeral home.

The moat is not sensitive data volume. It is a reusable, provider-neutral library of authority-aware work patterns, minimum-necessary handoff manifests, verified receipts, exceptions, recovery paths, and viewer-relative explanations.

## Target jobs

### Person planning ahead

"Help me leave a clear map of my digital life and wishes so the right people know what matters and where to start, without giving Passage my passwords or giving anyone access too early."

Required outcome:

- inventory the existence and purpose of accounts, subscriptions, digital property, photos, devices, and crypto custody without collecting secrets;
- choose preserve, transfer if allowed, memorialize, close, cancel, export, or ask for professional help;
- set up provider-native legacy controls where available;
- name a helper and alternate with purpose, category, timing, and revocation;
- leave a versioned Digital Continuity Brief and provider-native setup receipts.

### Trusted helper or friend

"I want to help someone prepare or handle one bounded step without pretending I own their decisions or exposing the rest of their life."

Required outcome:

- start in helper mode and invite the subject or authorized coordinator;
- distinguish information gathering from consent, legal authority, and execution;
- receive only assigned categories and actions;
- return questions, blockers, and receipts to the accountable owner;
- lose access when purpose expires or is revoked.

### Newly bereaved family coordinator

"Tell me which digital matters need attention, what can wait, what authority each provider needs, and what proof to save so I do not repeat the same story or accidentally destroy something important."

Required outcome:

- triage financial exposure, irreplaceable memories, public identity, active services, and low-risk cleanup separately;
- present one next action rather than an undifferentiated account list;
- prepare provider-specific packets for human review;
- keep submitted, acknowledged, completed, rejected, and needs-more-information distinct;
- name the recovery owner for every failure.

### Online-first or direct-cremation provider

"Give each family a calm, accountable aftercare handoff and visible progress without asking my care team to build another estate platform or surrender the service relationship."

Required outcome:

- provider-sponsored, provider-branded or clearly co-branded family entry;
- explicit boundary between disposition service and Passage continuity work;
- minimum-necessary handoff from the provider's accepted case;
- family-controlled continuation into digital and administrative aftercare;
- aggregate operational outcomes without exposing family content or selling leads.

### Death-positive educator, creator, or community organizer

"Give people a useful, accurate way to start a hard conversation without turning my trust or community into a sales funnel."

Required outcome:

- free educational artifacts that stand alone without account creation;
- citations, uncertainty, and clear professional-help boundaries;
- disclosed sponsorship or compensation;
- opt-in research separated from product signup;
- no promotion in communities whose rules prohibit it.

## Wedge sequence

The sequence is evidence-led. A later wedge does not become active merely because an earlier prototype exists.

### Wedge 1: Digital Continuity Brief

Begin with a non-custodial inventory and intent map. Record provider, account category, why it matters, desired outcome, named helper, provider-native tool status, authority needed, last review, and next action. Store no authentication secret.

Why first: it extends the Transition Brief and Continuity rail without requiring Passage to execute an external action. It can be tested with synthetic data before M4 identity/grants are live.

### Wedge 2: Help someone prepare

Add vicarious entry only after real identity, purpose grants, revocation, and viewer-relative projections are proven. The helper starts a draft or accepts one bounded assignment; the subject or authorized coordinator controls consent and release.

Why second: distribution can begin with the person who recognizes the need, but authority cannot be inferred from concern or relationship.

### Wedge 3: Provider-native setup and after-death action packets

Prepare instructions and manifests for Apple, Google, Meta, password-manager emergency access, subscriptions, cloud-photo preservation, and custodial crypto claims. A human reviews and uses the provider's official process. Passage records only the minimum receipt and exception.

Why third: it turns planning into verified continuity while preserving provider authority. Live submission, document delivery, or account action stays behind M6 integration, legal, privacy, security, and Production gates.

### Wedge 4: Online-first provider-sponsored distribution

Test one region and one provider type with a bounded, disclosed continuity benefit after an accepted arrangement. The selected provider remains responsible for disposition; Passage owns the permitted continuity workflow and proof return.

Why fourth: NFDA and provider materials establish digital arrangement behavior, but Passage must first prove that it reduces follow-up and improves family continuity rather than adding another portal.

### Wedge 5: Creator and community education

Co-create plain-language tools about digital continuity, helping a friend, provider-native legacy controls, and questions to ask. Education may lead to an optional Passage start, but the artifact must remain useful without signup.

Why fifth: trust must precede conversion. Community partnerships should scale only after independent comprehension, privacy, and non-exploitation review.

## Ethical distribution doctrine

1. **No grief arbitrage.** No countdowns, fear copy, hidden urgency, preselected purchases, or retargeting based on a death or health signal.
2. **No lead marketplace.** No commission ranking, paid placement, sale of family data, or routing to the highest bidder. Flat provider sponsorship must be disclosed and unrelated to family/provider selection.
3. **No community capture.** Death Cafe rules prohibit leading participants toward a product; Passage must not market, recruit, or collect leads there. Other communities require explicit organizer consent and visible sponsorship.
4. **No impersonation.** "Helping" never means signing in as the person, accepting terms for them, or claiming executor/fiduciary authority.
5. **No secret custody by default.** Passage does not request passwords, passkeys, seed phrases, private keys, recovery codes, or full payment credentials.
6. **Provider-native truth.** Show the official provider process, last verified date, required documents, submitted state, receipt, and exception. Never promise that a custodian will release, transfer, memorialize, or close an account.
7. **Minimum necessary proof.** Save that an action was requested or completed, by whom, when, under what authority, and who can see it; do not copy sensitive content into an event record.
8. **Editorial independence.** Creator and educator partners disclose compensation and retain the right to state limitations or criticism. Passage supplies claims and sources, not scripted endorsements.
9. **Professional boundaries.** Legal, tax, financial, security, mental-health, medical, funeral-director, and fiduciary judgments stay with qualified people and applicable owner gates.
10. **Accessible choice.** Direct acquisition must support religious, nonreligious, culturally specific, disability, language, and low-digital-confidence needs. "Death positive" is an optional framing, not the brand voice for every person.

## Risks and barriers

| Risk | Why it matters | Required mitigation or gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mistaken authority | A friend, family member, executor, account designee, and fiduciary may have different rights. | Identity, purpose, provenance, authority type, expiry, revocation, provider requirement, and denial must be explicit. |
| Credential or key loss | Passwords and crypto private keys create catastrophic security and irreversibility risk. | Non-custodial design; provider-native tools; qualified security/legal review before any exception. |
| Custodian fragmentation | Provider policies, available data, time limits, documents, and outcomes differ and change. | Versioned provider playbooks with source, jurisdiction, last verified date, receipt, and exception owner. |
| Legal/jurisdiction variance | RUFADAA adoption, probate, property, privacy, communications content, and fiduciary powers vary. | Educational language only until jurisdiction-specific expert review; no legal-access promise. |
| Destructive action | Closing an account may destroy photos, messages, subscriptions, funds, or identity evidence. | Preserve-before-close prompt, review boundary, explicit consequence, stronger confirmation, and correction/recovery where possible. |
| Crypto irreversibility and fraud | Wrong transfers and leaked keys may be unrecoverable. | Inventory/custodian route only in early V5; no seed phrase/private key custody or transfer execution. |
| Provider channel conflict | Funeral homes may perceive direct acquisition or online providers as disintermediation. | Consumer-selected provider, neutral presentation, no commission ranking, shared outcome evidence, and region-by-region trust proof. |
| Exploitative acquisition | People in grief have reduced attention and high vulnerability. | No dark patterns, grief targeting, covert endorsements, or community promotion; independent ethics/comprehension review. |
| Creator reputation transfer | Creator trust can hide commercial bias or unsupported claims. | Disclosure, source packet, editorial independence, claim review, and kill on misleading conversion behavior. |
| Support burden | Exceptions will require human interpretation across many providers. | Start with a tiny provider/category matrix, track recovery time and escalation rate, and kill expansion that cannot be supported safely. |

## Validation experiments

All experiments are research-only until separately scoped. Sending outreach, paying creators, starting ads, changing pricing, or using real bereaved-customer data requires the applicable owner gate. Synthetic prototypes must follow the normal PM -> UX -> Engineering -> QA -> Deploy train before becoming a reachable product surface.

### Experiment 1: digital continuity problem interviews

- Recruit 20 adults across planning-ahead, recent helper, executor/fiduciary, and online-first funeral consumer contexts.
- Use a category-sort exercise for subscriptions, social identity, cloud photos, devices, passwords, and crypto.
- Success: at least 14 identify three or more relevant categories and at least 12 prefer an inventory/next-action/proof tool over a second password vault.
- Kill or revise: fewer than 8 identify a recurring coordination problem, or most demand direct credential custody as the only value.

### Experiment 2: non-custodial Digital Continuity Brief comprehension

- Test a synthetic brief that contains account references, desired outcomes, helper scope, official provider routes, authority needed, receipt state, and recovery.
- Success: at least 16 of 20 can identify what Passage stores, what it does not store, who may act, and the next safe action without coaching; zero believe Passage holds passwords or guarantees access.
- Kill or revise: any participant would paste a password/seed phrase into the proposed fields, or more than 2 confuse a helper with legal authority.

### Experiment 3: vicarious entry

- Compare `I am planning for myself`, `I am helping someone prepare`, and `Someone has died` entry language in a consent prototype.
- Success: at least 8 of 10 helper-mode participants correctly invite or request scope without impersonation, can name what remains pending approval, and understand how access ends.
- Kill or revise: helper mode increases false-authority answers, hidden sharing, or subject confusion versus the self-start control.

### Experiment 4: provider-sponsored aftercare discovery

- Interview 8 operators across independent funeral homes, online-first cremation providers, and multi-location providers using one bounded aftercare concept.
- Success: at least 3 identify a named workflow owner, measurable follow-up burden, acceptable family handoff point, and willingness to test a nonexclusive pilot; at least 2 are online-first/direct-cremation operators.
- Kill or revise: fewer than 2 identify operational value, or interest depends on lead resale, commission ranking, broad family-data access, or replacing professional responsibility.

### Experiment 5: creator/community ethics council

- Review claims, language, sponsorship, opt-in, and no-promotion boundaries with 8 death-positive educators, doulas, librarians, community organizers, or rights advocates.
- Success: at least 6 rate the standalone education as useful and non-exploitative; at least 4 would test or critique it under disclosed terms; no Death Cafe promotion is proposed.
- Kill or revise: most perceive grief harvesting, institutional capture, false legal certainty, or forced signup.

### Experiment 6: synthetic provider-playbook proof

- Model Apple, Google, Meta, one password-manager emergency-access path, one subscription closure, and custodial crypto claim using synthetic data and official sources.
- Success: each path has a distinct authority requirement, prepared/submitted/acknowledged/completed state, minimum receipt, destructive-action warning, and recovery owner; no secret enters the record.
- Kill or revise: the common model erases meaningful provider differences or cannot explain an exception without exposing sensitive content.

## Program success and kill criteria

V5 may advance from research to a PM-scoped design packet only when:

- Experiments 1-3 meet their comprehension and authority thresholds.
- At least one acquisition hypothesis meets its channel-specific threshold without paid placement or data resale.
- The product can remain non-custodial for authentication secrets and crypto keys.
- The V5 model reuses the same continuity, task, proof, event, grant, and recovery rails rather than creating a parallel estate backend.
- Security, privacy, legal, and ethics reviewers can name a bounded initial category set and prohibited fields.
- Cycle 8 and the prerequisite M3/M4 gates have passed before implementation scope is proposed.

Hold or kill the V5 direction when:

- value depends on passwords, seed phrases, impersonation, unsupported executor claims, or autonomous transfers;
- acquisition depends on covert grief targeting, creator trust rental, Death Cafe promotion, lead resale, or commission-ranked providers;
- provider demand exists only if Passage owns the funeral relationship or hides sponsorship;
- exception/support burden makes truthful recovery economically or operationally untenable;
- the experience cannot explain authority, audience, saved proof, destructive consequences, and recovery in plain language at 1440, 390, and 360;
- V5 would delay the M3-M6 evidence gates or fragment V4 into a second network.

## Anti-features

Passage V5 is not:

- a password, passkey, seed-phrase, private-key, or recovery-code vault;
- a deceased-person impersonation or login service;
- a digital executor, law firm, fiduciary, crypto custodian, or financial advisor;
- an AI resurrection, dead-person chatbot, voice clone, avatar, or automated posthumous posting system;
- a universal account-closing switch;
- a scraper of bank statements, inboxes, devices, or social profiles to infer accounts without explicit permission;
- a public memorial network or grief social feed;
- a funeral-home comparison marketplace ranked by commission;
- a lead reseller or family-data broker;
- a creator affiliate scheme disguised as education;
- a commercial activation inside Death Cafe or another no-promotion community;
- a substitute for funeral directors, estate professionals, platform custodians, or provider-specific authority checks.

## Exact UX handoff for a later design gate

When the roadmap permits a V5 UX packet, UI/UX Review must produce a field-level and state-level contract before Engineering begins:

1. Three situation entries: planning for myself; helping someone prepare; handling matters after a death.
2. A visible role boundary that distinguishes subject, helper, family coordinator, executor/fiduciary, provider designee, and professional without asking users to self-certify authority they do not have.
3. One Digital Continuity Brief hierarchy: what matters now; account category and provider; desired outcome; authority needed; owner; waiting party; official route; last verified date; saved receipt; audience; failure recovery.
4. Prohibited-field prevention for passwords, passkeys, PINs, secret answers, recovery codes, seed phrases, private keys, and full payment credentials, including clear redirection to provider-native secure tools.
5. Separate states for draft, waiting for consent, ready for human review, submitted outside Passage, acknowledged, needs more information, completed, rejected, expired, revoked, failed, and recovered.
6. A preserve-before-close warning for photos, messages, identity, licensed media, funds, and other potentially destructive outcomes.
7. Helper invitation, accept, decline, revoke, expire, and return-to-owner flows with minimum-necessary projection and no adjacent account browsing.
8. Sponsor and creator disclosures that are understandable before signup and do not change provider ranking or user choice.
9. Seven-question plain-language comprehension at 1440, 390, and 360, including explicit uncertainty: Passage can prepare and record; the provider decides access or completion.
10. Loading, empty, denied, wrong-person, insufficient-authority, provider-policy-changed, destructive-action, partial-completion, and escalation states with one safe next action and named recovery owner.

No V5 Engineering handoff is authorized by this strategy document.
